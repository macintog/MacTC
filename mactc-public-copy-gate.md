# MacTC Public Copy Gate

Version: 1.0  
Status: Internal standard  
Scope: All public-facing non-EULA copy for MacTC, including website, storefronts, screenshots, onboarding text, docs, FAQs, release notes, blog posts, social posts, email campaigns, support macros, and in-app explanatory copy outside the license agreement.

---

## 1. Purpose

This document is the standing rule set for any public copy that describes MacTC.

Its purpose is to ensure all public-facing language is:

- truthful,
- specific,
- non-misleading,
- properly qualified,
- consistent across channels,
- calm and readable,
- and free of accidental promises about safety, protection, longevity, warranty impact, or guaranteed thermal outcomes.

This standard applies to both:

1. generation of new copy, and
2. review or validation of existing copy.

If any rule in this document conflicts with a more specific legal, product, or engineering instruction issued later, the more specific instruction controls.

---

## 2. Core Product Framing

MacTC is a Mac thermal-control utility that changes fan behavior to pursue lower operating temperatures, typically at the cost of increased fan activity and increased fan noise.

That framing is always acceptable.

The following points are always true enough to say publicly unless engineering later restricts them:

- MacTC changes or influences fan behavior.
- Its goal is to pursue lower temperatures.
- Lower temperatures usually come at the cost of more fan activity and more fan noise.
- Outcomes depend on hardware, firmware, macOS behavior, workloads, sensors, ambient conditions, and other factors outside the developer's full control.
- Better results cannot be guaranteed in every circumstance.

## 3. Tone Rules

### 3.1 General tone

Public non-EULA copy must sound like product communication, not a contract.

Use:

- plain English,
- short sentences,
- specific nouns,
- calm verbs,
- and ordinary product language.

Avoid sounding defensive, dramatic, threatening, or lawyerly unless legal language is necessary for accuracy.

### 3.2 Required tone characteristics

Public copy should be:

- direct,
- restrained,
- technically literate,
- honest about uncertainty,
- and easy for a non-lawyer customer to understand.

### 3.3 Avoid in non-EULA copy unless critical

Avoid these unless the surface is specifically legal or warning-oriented:

- indemnify
- hold harmless
- with all faults
- to the fullest extent permitted by law
- consequential damages
- punitive damages
- merchantability
- non-infringement
- assumption of risk

Preferred replacements in ordinary copy:

- "cannot guarantee"
- "may be less effective than intended"
- "may worsen thermal behavior in some circumstances"
- "use only if you understand and accept that limitation"

## 4. Always-Allowed Messaging Patterns

The following sentence structures are safe defaults.

### 4.1 Product description

- "MacTC gives advanced users more control over fan behavior in pursuit of lower operating temperatures."
- "MacTC is designed for users who prefer a cooler system at the cost of more fan noise."
- "MacTC changes fan behavior to pursue lower temperatures, typically by increasing fan activity earlier or more aggressively than default behavior."

### 4.2 Limitation / uncertainty

- "MacTC is intended to improve thermal control, but it cannot guarantee better results in every circumstance."
- "Thermal behavior depends on hardware, firmware, operating system behavior, workloads, sensors, and ambient conditions."
- "In rare or unforeseen cases, MacTC may be less effective than intended or may worsen thermal behavior."

### 4.3 User suitability

- "MacTC is for users who understand the tradeoff between lower temperatures and more fan noise."
- "Advanced modes should be enabled only by users who understand and accept that no fan-control policy can guarantee better outcomes in every scenario."

### 4.4 Documentation explanations

- "This setting makes fan response more aggressive."
- "This may reduce temperatures under sustained load, but it may also increase noise and power use."
- "Results vary by Mac model, workload, ambient temperature, and system state."

## 5. Mandatory Disclosure Rules

These are content gates. If public copy discusses benefits, features, modes, or outcomes, the required disclosures below must appear somewhere reasonably close to the claim.

### 5.1 If copy mentions lower temperatures

It must also communicate at least one of the following:

- that the tradeoff is more fan noise or fan activity,
- that results vary,
- or that better outcomes are not guaranteed in every circumstance.

### 5.2 If copy mentions safety, protection, or hardware care

It must not imply certainty.

It must include a clear qualifier such as:

- "cannot guarantee better thermal outcomes in every circumstance"
- "no software-based fan policy can guarantee protection against every edge case"
- "outcomes depend on factors outside the developer's control"

### 5.3 If copy mentions advanced, manual, or aggressive modes

It must state that:

- the mode may behave differently from Apple defaults,
- outcomes are not guaranteed,
- and in rare or unforeseen cases the mode may worsen thermal behavior.

### 5.4 If copy mentions compatibility

Never say or imply universal compatibility.

Use:

- "supported Macs"
- "compatible models"
- "supported versions of macOS"
- "see the compatibility list"

### 5.5 If copy mentions future updates

Do not imply permanence.

Use:

- "behavior may change with future macOS or firmware updates"
- "support for future versions is not guaranteed until validated"

## 6. Hard-Prohibited Claims

Any occurrence of the following claim categories is an automatic fail unless specifically cleared by legal and engineering with written substantiation.

### 6.1 Safety guarantees

Do not say or imply:

- "safe"
- "safer"
- "safest"
- "protects your Mac"
- "prevents damage"
- "prevents overheating"
- "keeps your hardware safe"
- "ensures safe temperatures"
- "guaranteed protection"

### 6.2 Warranty claims

Do not say or imply:

- "will not affect your warranty"
- "cannot void your warranty"
- "Apple-safe"
- "approved by Apple"
- "works within Apple's intended limits" unless this has been specifically reviewed and is literally accurate in context

### 6.3 Longevity claims

Do not say or imply:

- "extends hardware life"
- "extends Mac lifespan"
- "extends battery life" unless the meaning is runtime and that is explicitly stated and substantiated
- "reduces wear" unless narrowly tied to a particular measurable mechanism and qualified

### 6.4 Certainty / universal-outcome claims

Do not say or imply:

- "always"
- "never"
- "guaranteed"
- "in all cases"
- "in every case"
- "cannot make things worse"
- "only improves thermals"
- "failsafe"
- "bulletproof"

### 6.5 Comparative claims about Apple defaults

Do not say or imply:

- "better than Apple's fan control"
- "fixes Apple's thermal management"
- "Apple's fan control is wrong"
- "Apple's fan curve is unsafe"

unless the statement is tightly scoped, factual, non-inflammatory, and supported by evidence.

Preferred pattern:

- "MacTC is for users who prefer a cooler system with more fan noise than the default behavior provides."

### 6.6 Hidden legal overreach in ordinary copy

Do not embed quasi-legal absolutes in marketing or docs, such as:

- "you assume all risk"
- "we are not responsible for anything"
- "use at your own risk" as a standalone substitute for actual explanation

Those may appear in legal materials, but public explanatory copy must explain the product reality, not rely on naked legal slogans.

## 7. Restricted Claims Requiring Qualification

These claims are allowed only if immediately qualified.

### 7.1 "Lower temperatures"

Allowed only with one of:

- "typically"
- "often"
- "under many workloads"
- "results vary"
- "cannot guarantee better results in every circumstance"

### 7.2 "Improves thermal control"

Allowed only if framed as intent or typical behavior.

Preferred:

- "intended to improve thermal control"
- "designed to improve thermal control under many conditions"

Avoid:

- "improves thermal control" without qualification

### 7.3 "More control"

Allowed, but do not let it imply omniscience or precision beyond what exists.

Avoid:

- "precise hardware control"
- "complete thermal control"
- "full control over Mac thermals"

### 7.4 "Supported"

Allowed only if support boundaries are real and documented.

Do not write:

- "supports macOS 13 and later" unless you have explicitly validated all covered versions you name
- "supports Apple silicon" if important model limitations exist and are not mentioned nearby

## 8. Mandatory Qualifier Vocabulary

At least one of these ideas should appear whenever copy touches claims that could otherwise sound like a promise.

Preferred qualifiers:

- intended to
- designed to
- typically
- often
- under many conditions
- results vary
- depends on
- cannot guarantee
- in some circumstances
- in rare or unforeseen cases
- may be less effective than intended
- may worsen thermal behavior

Avoid weaker evasive hedges that sound slippery:

- sort of
- kind of
- probably
- should
n- likely safe
- ought to

## 9. Channel-Specific Rules

### 9.1 Website hero / top-of-page copy

Keep it simple. One benefit, one tradeoff, one qualifier.

Recommended pattern:

1. what MacTC does,
2. who it is for,
3. the tradeoff,
4. the no-guarantee qualifier.

### 9.2 Feature pages

Every feature claim must be paired with:

- the main tradeoff,
- the main variability drivers,
- and the strongest non-alarmist qualifier needed for that feature.

### 9.3 Documentation

Docs may be more technical, but must not become more absolute.

If the docs explain aggressive modes, unsupported states, or troubleshooting, they should say plainly when:

- a setting may increase noise,
- a setting may increase power use,
- a setting may not help under every workload,
- a setting may behave differently after system updates.

### 9.4 Release notes

Do not convert bug fixes into promises.

Allowed:

- "improves behavior on some Apple silicon laptops during sleep/wake transitions"

Not allowed:

- "fixes sleep/wake thermals"
- "resolves overheating"

### 9.5 Support replies

Support must not make custom promises that conflict with the public gate.

Never say:

- "don't worry, it can't hurt anything"
- "this will definitely protect your Mac"
- "this is safer than Apple's default"

Use:

- "the app is intended to improve thermal control, but outcomes can vary by model, workload, and system conditions"
- "we can't guarantee better results in every case"

### 9.6 Social posts

Short format is not an excuse to omit essential qualification if the claim is strong.

If space is limited, reduce the claim rather than dropping the qualifier.

## 10. Required Disclosures by Surface

### 10.1 Product page

Must include all of the following somewhere above the fold or reasonably near the main feature statement:

- MacTC changes fan behavior.
- It pursues lower temperatures.
- The tradeoff is more fan activity and noise.
- Better outcomes are not guaranteed in every circumstance.

### 10.2 Purchase page

Must include or link conspicuously to:

- the EULA,
- the risk disclosure,
- and an affirmative assent mechanism.

### 10.3 First launch

Must state:

- what the app changes,
- the basic tradeoff,
- and that better outcomes cannot be guaranteed in every circumstance.

### 10.4 Advanced mode enablement

Must state:

- that behavior may differ significantly from defaults,
- that better outcomes are not guaranteed,
- and that rare or unforeseen cases may worsen thermal behavior.

## 13. Copy Review Checklist

Every piece of public copy must pass all of these questions.

### 13.1 Truthfulness

- Does every factual statement match the product as it exists today?
- Is every comparison supportable?
- Is every version, model, and compatibility statement current?

### 13.2 Missing-information test

- Could a reasonable reader come away thinking MacTC guarantees safety, protection, or better outcomes?
- If yes, add clearer qualifiers or reduce the claim.

### 13.3 Tradeoff test

- If the copy mentions lower temperatures, does it also mention fan noise, fan activity, or another real tradeoff?

### 13.4 Certainty test

- Does the copy contain any absolute word like always, never, guaranteed, or safe?
- If yes, remove or rewrite.

### 13.5 Warranty test

- Does the copy imply anything about Apple's warranty or approval?
- If yes, remove unless specifically reviewed.

### 13.6 Longevity test

- Does the copy imply extended hardware life, reduced wear, or better long-term safety?
- If yes, remove unless specifically reviewed and substantiated.

### 13.7 Tone test

- Does the copy sound like a product explanation rather than a legal disclaimer?
- If no, simplify and de-legalize without weakening the substance.

### 13.8 Consistency test

- Does the copy materially conflict with the EULA, the first-launch warning, advanced-mode warning, or the docs?
- If yes, rewrite until aligned.

---

## 14. Review Outcomes

Each review should end in one of four outcomes.

- **Pass** — usable as written.
- **Pass with edits** — minor revisions required.
- **Fail: overclaim** — makes unsupported or overly absolute claims.
- **Fail: missing qualifier** — claim may be supportable but lacks required context.

## 15. Standing Instruction Block for Future Copy Generation

Use the block below as a reusable system prompt, author brief, or validator instruction.

> Generate or revise public-facing non-EULA copy for MacTC.
> 
> Hard requirements:
> - Use plain English, not legalese, unless legal wording is absolutely necessary.
> - Describe MacTC as a utility that changes fan behavior to pursue lower temperatures, typically at the cost of increased fan noise and fan activity.
> - Never imply safety, guaranteed protection, guaranteed better outcomes, longer hardware life, Apple approval, or warranty neutrality.
> - Whenever the copy mentions lower temperatures, protection, advanced control, or benefits, include a clear qualifier such as results vary, cannot guarantee better outcomes in every circumstance, or in rare or unforeseen cases may worsen thermal behavior.
> - Avoid absolute words like always, never, guaranteed, safe, fail-safe, harmless, or better than Apple.
> - Keep the tone calm, precise, and product-like.
> - If the copy is too short to carry the needed qualifier honestly, reduce the claim.
> 
> Validation rule:
> If a sentence could cause a reasonable reader to believe MacTC guarantees better safety, protection, longevity, or outcomes, rewrite it until that implication is removed.
