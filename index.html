THE BACKSTORY

Built after watching long GPU jobs get slower in real time

The story behind MacTC is not "fans are too quiet." It is "this machine stayed pleasant while a real workload quietly gave up speed." That is a much narrower and more useful reason for the product to exist.

WHAT I SAW

Long runs in LM Studio would start fast, then fade.

On one M4 Max MacBook Pro, repeated local model runs in LM Studio made the slowdown obvious: tokens per second would drift down several minutes into sustained work, even after the fans had already started climbing.

Checking the machine with mactop made the pattern easier to understand. GPU clocks began near full speed, then stepped down as heat soaked in. On that system, a paid-for 40-core GPU could behave more like a much smaller one once the session had been hot for a while.

That is not a universal claim about every Mac. It is the workload shape that pushed MacTC into existence: long, heavy sessions where staying quiet can eventually cost throughput.

WHAT MACTC TRIES INSTEAD

Be as quiet as practical, but not at the cost of avoidable slowdown.

Fixed fan percentages were often the wrong tradeoff in both directions: noisier than necessary when the machine was comfortable, but still too late once sustained GPU heat had already built up.

MacTC defers to the system by default, then tries to front-run rising heat debt with more fan earlier when the workload looks like it may outrun Apple's quieter preference.

And when throttling begins, it can push much harder, then back down toward the lowest fan level that still holds performance. Results vary, and better outcomes are not guaranteed in every circumstance.

WHY IT FEELS DIFFERENT

It is not just "fan percent by temperature" with a different paint job

System-first by default

MacTC does not assume Apple's default behavior is wrong for everyone. It watches for rising power, heat, and soak before it becomes more aggressive.

Earlier intervention when heat debt is building

The goal is to front-run the kind of sustained load that can drag clocks down later, while still accepting that results vary and better outcomes are not guaranteed in every circumstance.

Ramp instead of slam

When MacTC does need more fan, it aims to climb over a few seconds and back off once performance settles, rather than living at full blast all the time.
